The Newman-Peacock Score

The Newman-Peacock score is used to estimate the probability of successfully performing external cephalic version (ECV) in the event of fetal breech presentation. It was defined in a 1993 study of ECV performance in a single institution (Newman RB, Peacock BS, Vandorsten JP, Hunt HH. Predicting success of external cephalic version. Am J Obstet Gynecol. 1993;169(2 Pt 1):245-9), which identified five factors that predominantly affect the likelihood of successful version (estimated fetal weight, parity, placenta location, station, and cervical dilation).

The CART Score

The CART score is used to estimate the probability of successfully performing external cephalic version (ECV) in the event of fetal breech presentation. It was created in a 2017 study of 1,253 pregnant women with a fetus in breech presentation (Hutton EK, Simioni JC, Thabane L. Predictors of success of external cephalic version and cephalic presentation at birth among 1253 women with non-cephalic presentation using logistic regression and classification tree analyses. Acta Obstet Gynecol Scand. 2017;96(8):1012-1020). It used a classification and regression tree (CART) analysis to identify several factors highly correlated with ECV success, and organized these factors into a clinically-usable algorithm.
