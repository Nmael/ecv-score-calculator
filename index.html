The Newman-Peacock Score

The Newman-Peacock score is used to estimate the probability of successfully performing external cephalic version (ECV) in the event of fetal breech presentation. It was defined in a 1993 study of ECV performance in a single institution (Newman RB, Peacock BS, Vandorsten JP, Hunt HH. Predicting success of external cephalic version. Am J Obstet Gynecol. 1993;169(2 Pt 1):245-9), which identified five factors that predominantly affect the likelihood of successful version (estimated fetal weight, parity, placenta location, station, and cervical dilation).
